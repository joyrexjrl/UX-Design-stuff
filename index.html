Transportation

Cruise Ship

Cruise ships travel to Taniti year round but are most popular during the summer months when sea life is more active. Cost can vary depending on your departure location. Check your local travel agencies for more information about cruises to Taniti.

By Air

Flying to Taniti is by far the faster method, with the local international airport servicing both international, local, and farm duster flights this is by far the most common method of travel to and from Taniti. Locals are working out the blueprints for visitors to eventual drive to Taniti, however this is looking to be a little more difficult than once assumed.

Island Travel

Local Ship

Local ships are a great way to travel around the islands, with the best views of the local transportation options. Some ship venders even offer door side pickup, check your tour guide for cost and regulations.

By Bus

Do you have a large group of people traveling with you? Then take our busses to your next destination, busses run from 6am to 10pm every day and stop at all hotels attractions across the island. Bus travel cost is included in the cost of the hotels and can be used any time during the duration of your stay.

Scuba

Scuba Guide

Meet our premier scuba expert, Dr. Hastoophers D Ghoti. With 4 years (almost a lifetime for a fish) of scuba experience and extensive knowledge of the area, divers will be in good fins with Hastoophers as the guide.

Scuba gear can be rented for $200 - $250 depending on availability. Tanks are rented out for $130 for a pair of tanks.

Scuba certifications can be acquired for $500 and is accepted worldwide.

Shark Friends

Our shark friend’s love being around people and the beaches! Never fear though as our local sharks have all been trained, they are aware that humans are friends, not food. Some have even turned to veganism and only eat seaweed.

Orange Fin Dancers

Taniti proudly presents our very own Orange Fin Dancers! While in the land of scuba diving, there is a good chance the dancers will come by and give you a show of a life time (assuming the shark friends are not around). Trained in synchronized dancing seemingly from birth, the dancers are sure to mesmerize you with wiggles from the sea.

About

Taniti was founded in 1312 by Lord Montique Longbottom III Esq Jr. Montique (for now on being referred to as Lord) was born in the Nijenheim region in Zeist in the Netherlands around 1287 to Bread maker (and winner) Mary, and town Philosopher/ Local Thinker, John Longbottom IX who has famously coined the word “why” when in public wife Mary asked John to get a job, to which John replied with the most deeply profound reply of why. To this day the smartest minds across the globe cannot absolutely answer the question of why.

Lord would travel with his parents to their summer home in the Bahamas almost every year, however after the unexpected death of his father when asked to move in haste to which he replied with his famous response of why, was struck in the head by a falling brick. His mother having to bake more bread then before was unable to afford yearly trips to their summer home. Lord spent the remainder of his early years trying to figure out a way to have his very own Bahamas style summer home.

Luck struck one year when Lord signed up as a fisherman off the coast of current day Somalia, where they didn't fish for literal fish, but more asking for handouts from other actual fisherman (aka stole money from them). After a swift and decisive mutiny between him and the captain of the raft, Lord sailed to an unknown location just west of current day Somalias shore, which would become our beloved Taniti.

Taniti was said to be named after Lords pet pidgin that would fly into buildings, who was once thought to have been the cause of the brick that laid waste to Lords father John. Taniti, (the pet bird) who shortly after arriving to the island, was eaten by the critically endangered local bird species known as the Piphit. The Piphit, the last of its kind, unfortunately choked on Taniti and died, as a result becoming Lords first meal on the island. The local dish is said to be derived from this bird stuffed bird meal, known as the hamburger.

Proposed flag of Taniti.

Over the next few years Lord built housing and hotels for the locals, who eventually named him the leader of the land. Shortly after this time tourist began showing up in droves from all over to see the marvel that is, Taniti. In 1314 Taniti was recognized as a legitimate country by the common wealth of Atlantus, which shortly after sank in a random flood. In 1315 Taniti created a “foundation of islands” called “Nay-Toe” which was designed to protect the lands from random flood attacks so they would not suffer the same fate as Atlantus.

In 1320 Lord decided it was time for his land to have a flag that would be adored worldwide, having missed many weeks of sleep the final design was reviled to Taniti. After some discussion with the locals it was determined that the flags design was already in use by France for their war time battle standards. To this day a flag has yet to be drawn up.

FAQ

Q: What is the best way to get to Taniti?

A: There is no real best way to arrive to Taniti, your decision might vary on what time of year it is. During the summer months, if your travel time allows, arrival by cruise ship would allow more sights then simply flying in. Flying is always available any time of year.

Q: What are the laws and rules for public smoking in Taniti?

A: Rules and laws for smoking closely follow the same regulations as in north America. There are no smoking areas in restaurants, hotels have regulated outdoor smoking areas and smoking in the rooms is not allowed. Here in Taniti there are designated smoking areas in and around public areas such as beaches, hiking trails, town areas etc to help cut down on potential fires and to keep the area and air clean. Any changes to these rules are clearly posted on a location by location basis, for example some night clubs allow indoor smoking.

Q: What are laws for pets in Taniti?

A: Pets depending on the type, are allowed. Dogs are allowed on the beaches and in some public buildings. Beaches all have pet locations where the owners can gather any supplies needed to clean up after their pet. If owners don't clean up after their pet, fines will be issued and paid through the pet owners hotel cost. Cats are not allowed on the island at all due to potential issues with Tanitis natural wild life. Under no circumstances are pigeons allowed on the island.

Q: What are the laws for drinking in public?

A: Public drinking is not allowed anywhere on the island, some restaurants and night clubs do have patios and outdoor areas where public drinking is allowed.

Q: Is driving on the beaches allowed?

A: Driving street legal vehicles are not allowed due to natural beach wildlife. Most Taniti beaches do have locations where beach bikes and other allowed vehicles can be rented.
